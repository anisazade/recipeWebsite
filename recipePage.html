Cheese Revioli

By Anna Glinton • July 2022

There’s something magical about homemade ravioli. The soft, delicate pasta cradling a creamy, cheesy filling feels like a warm hug from the past—a dish that carries generations of tradition and love.

I remember the first time I tried making ravioli with my grandmother in her tiny, flour-dusted kitchen. She would hum softly as she rolled out the pasta dough, her hands moving with the precision of someone who had done this a thousand times before.

"The secret," she would say, "is in the filling. You can have the best pasta in the world, but if the filling isn't perfect, it won’t taste like home." And so, we made the filling—a blend of ricotta, Parmesan, and a hint of nutmeg, with just enough mozzarella to give it that irresistible stretch. I remember sneaking a spoonful before it even made it into the pasta squares, savoring the creamy, slightly tangy, and utterly indulgent taste.

As the ravioli boiled, the kitchen filled with a rich, buttery aroma. We topped them with a simple sage-infused brown butter sauce, letting the nutty, slightly crispy sage leaves bring out the cheese’s depth. Each bite melted in my mouth—soft pasta, creamy cheese, and the warmth of a dish made with care.

Now, every time I make ravioli, I remember that afternoon with my grandmother ,where food was more than just sustenance—it was a way to share love, tradition, and memories.

Instructions:
  • Make the pasta dough
    1. On a clean surface, make a flour well and crack the eggs into the center
    2. Add salt and olive oil, then gradually mix the flour into the eggs using a fork.
    3. Knead the dough for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If too dry, add water one tablespoon at a time
    4. Wrap in plastic and let it rest for 30 minutes.
